## 1. Purpose

This document answers the question: **"If a B2B/SaaS client signs at tier X, what do Yarden and Adam actually deliver each month — and does it fit inside the founder-hour ceiling for that tier?"**

It is NOT pricing, brand, or design. It IS the delivery contract.

**Tier names are locked.** Dollar pricing is not in scope here — see `BUSINESS_MODEL.md`. If a price moves, scope does not move with it — these are the deliverables a tier name implies.

## 2. Capacity primer — hard ceiling

From `BUSINESS_MODEL.md` §Capacity Model:

| Tier | Founder-hrs/mo (delivery only) | Onboarding (one-time, weeks 1–4) |
|------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Starter | ~10 hrs | ~8 hrs |
| Growth | ~28 hrs | ~20 hrs |
| Scale | ~50 hrs | ~36 hrs |

> "Delivery only" excludes sales, automation build, admin (CBO budget: 100 hrs/mo overhead across both founders). Onboarding is **above** the monthly cadence and is amortized into the first month's hours-load — see §4.

**Anything in this doc that exceeds the tier's monthly ceiling is flagged in §8 — Capacity Flags.** Stage 1 is mostly manual; the math reflects that. As Adam's automation lands (CTO track), hours-per-line will drop and either (a) capacity ceiling rises or (b) scope per tier expands at the same price. **For Wave 1, we hold scope and let margin compound.**

## 3. Tier deliverables — exact scope

### 3.1 Starter — "Get found. Get credible."

**Promise:** The foundations of a coordinated B2B digital footprint. Not the full swarm — the swarm's first three legs.

**Target client (from `BUSINESS_MODEL.md`):** Early-stage B2B/SaaS, <10 employees, first time building systematic digital presence.

| # | Service | Monthly cadence | Hrs/mo (est.) | DoD ref |
|---|---------|-----------------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | GEO / AI-search visibility — ongoing | Schema validated, llms.txt monitored, AI-citation tracker run | 0.5 | DoD-1 |
| 2 | SEO content engine | 2 long-form pieces/mo (~1,200–1,800 words each) | 5.0 | DoD-2 |
| 3 | LinkedIn organic (company page + 1 founder) | 4 posts/mo (mix of insight, reshare, founder voice) | 2.0 | DoD-3 |
| 4 | Rank tracking + monthly report | Auto-pulled, human-written 1-page summary | 1.0 | DoD-4 |
| 5 | Brand monitoring | Mentions + competitor signal digest, monthly | 0.5 | DoD-5 |
| 6 | Client async update + Q&A | 1 monthly written update; ad-hoc Slack reply window | 1.0 | DoD-6 |
| | **Total** | | **10.0** | Fits ceiling ✓ |

**Explicitly NOT in Starter** (no exceptions without scope-creep guard): paid ads, YouTube, short-form video, email lifecycle, landing pages, digital PR, case studies, founder personal-brand multi-author, weekly calls.

**Stretch into Growth at month 4+ if:** client wants paid ads, email lifecycle, or landing-page builds.

## 4. Phased onboarding playbook (60–90 days)

Same three-phase shape across every tier — depth varies by tier. The phasing is the promise: **we do NOT light up 20 channels in week one.**

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

| Activity | Starter | Growth | Scale |
|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Brand intake (voice canon, ICP, JTBD, competitor read) | 2 hrs | 3 hrs | 4 hrs |
| Technical audit (SEO + GEO + site health + tracking) | 2 hrs | 3 hrs | 4 hrs |
| Schema + llms.txt + AI-crawler access setup | 1 hr | 1.5 hrs | 2 hrs |
| Rank-tracking + reporting dashboard wiring | 1 hr | 1.5 hrs | 2 hrs |
| Brand monitoring alerts setup | 0.5 hr | 0.5 hr | 1 hr |
| Content voice calibration (test-piece + founder sign-off) | 1.5 hrs | 3 hrs | 4 hrs |
| Paid ads pixel + UTM + conversion setup | — | 2 hrs | 2 hrs |
| Email platform + lifecycle template install | — | 2 hrs | 3 hrs |
| CRM integration + lead-scoring v0 | — | — | 6 hrs |
| Attribution model v0 | — | — | 4 hrs |
| Review-site profile claims (G2/Capterra/Trustpilot) | — | — | 2 hrs |
| Kickoff call + 30-day plan walkthrough | 1 hr | 2 hrs | 2 hrs |
| **One-time total** | **~8 hrs** | **~20 hrs** | **~36 hrs** |

> Phase 1 hours sit **on top of** Month-1 ongoing delivery — flag in client comms. Month 1 will feel light on outputs and heavy on setup; that's the promise: "we build the foundation that makes everything else compound" (`POSITIONING.md` §5 Step 1).

### Phase 2 — Content Engine (Weeks 4–8)

Output starts moving. Each tier turns on its content cadence:

- **Starter:** SEO content engine live (2 pieces shipped by W8), LinkedIn cadence live, first monthly report.
- **Growth:** All Starter + email lifecycle live (welcome seq sending), paid ads live (low-spend test), first landing page experiment, first founder-led post series shipped.
- **Scale:** All Growth + first YouTube video shot, first newsletter edition shipped, CRM workflows running, first lead magnet drafted.

### Phase 3 — Amplification (Weeks 8–12)

The swarm is fully active. Optimization begins.

- **Starter:** GEO citability brief written, second monthly report shows first ranking movement, content engine at full cadence, recommendation: discuss Growth upgrade if pipeline signal is positive.
- **Growth:** Digital PR program live (first placements landed or in flight), paid ads optimized off real conversion data, CRO experiments shipped with results, case-study interview booked for one early-converted lead.
- **Scale:** Multi-channel attribution producing usable signal, first webinar promoted, review-site review-outreach campaign running, full reporting cadence (weekly strategy + monthly report) locked in.

**End of Day 90 deliverable for every tier:** a written "where we are, what we learned, what we ship next 90 days" review. Required, not optional.

## 5. Definition of Done — per core service

Every monthly deliverable must pass these. **"Delivered, not generic"** is the bar. Failing DoD = work is rejected internally before it leaves the swarm.

| DoD # | Service | "Delivered, not generic" means |
|-------|---------|-------------------------------|
| DoD-1 | **GEO / AI-search visibility** | Schema validates clean in Google Rich Results test; llms.txt published and matches site sitemap; brand entity present in at least 2 of {Wikipedia, Crunchbase, LinkedIn Company, G2} with consistent description; monthly AI-citation check on 5 target queries shows tracked-mention deltas (not just "we ran it") |
| DoD-2 | **SEO content engine** | Topic chosen from a client-approved monthly editorial calendar (not random); each piece passes brand-voice check (`POSITIONING.md` §8 voice canon — direct, anti-generic, no buzzword list); each piece has GEO-aware structure (TL;DR + Q&A blocks for AI citability); each piece has explicit target keyword + secondary terms documented; published with internal links + schema |
| DoD-3 | **LinkedIn organic** | Each post tied to one of: insight, founder POV, customer story, or product update — never filler; each post has a hook line that doesn't begin with a buzzword; each post has a clear CTA or end-question (not "thoughts?"); company-page and founder-voice posts are written differently (not duplicated) |
| DoD-4 | **Reporting** | Auto-pulled data + **human commentary section** ("what changed, what we'd do about it") — never a screenshot dump; tracks goals defined at kickoff, not vanity metrics; flags any metric that went the wrong direction with a stated next-action |
| DoD-5 | **Brand monitoring** | Real alerts (not "no mentions" digests); each surfaced mention is categorized {positive/neutral/negative/competitor-name-drop} and routed (positive→amplify; negative→flag; competitor→note); monthly digest is ≤ 1 page |
| DoD-6 | **Client comms** | Update has 3 sections: what shipped, what we learned, what's next — never a hours-log; async reply within 1 business day during client hours window; calls have a written agenda sent ≥ 24h ahead |
| DoD-7 | **Paid ads** | Each campaign has a documented hypothesis (audience × creative × landing page), not "test more creatives"; CPL/CAC tracked against the client's documented baseline (not industry benchmark); ad copy passes the voice canon; ad spend never overruns the client-approved cap |
| DoD-8 | **Email lifecycle** | Every email serves one job (welcome, activation, nurture, win-back) — no Frankenstein emails; subject lines A/B tested when send size justifies; deliverability checked monthly (SPF/DKIM/DMARC valid, sender reputation tracked); unsubscribes routed and surfaced in report |
| DoD-9 | **Landing pages / CRO** | Each new page or experiment has a written hypothesis + success metric set BEFORE launch; experiments run to statistical confidence or to a written "kill it" date; learnings documented in a shared experiment log — failures count as deliverables |
| DoD-10 | **Digital PR / backlink** | Each placement is real, tracked by URL, and tied to a documented pitch angle; "we sent 50 cold emails" is not a deliverable — the deliverable is the published placement; toxic/spammy/PBN-style backlinks are explicitly off-limits |
| DoD-11 | **Founder-led personal-brand content** | Written FROM the founder (interview + draft + founder edit), not BY us as a ghostwritten generic post; voice audit by Yarden before publish; never reused company-page content with a name swap |
| DoD-12 | **Case study** | Real customer named (with permission) OR anonymized with industry/size, real problem, real numbers (or a written explanation if numbers aren't available yet); follows situation → approach → result format; reusable in landing-page, sales deck, and LinkedIn formats |
| DoD-13 | **YouTube** | Each video has a documented hook (first 8 seconds), thumbnail tested against ≥ 1 variant when in YouTube Studio Test mode, SEO title + description + chapters set, end-screen CTA defined, repurpose plan written before shoot |
| DoD-14 | **Short-form video** | Each clip stands alone (no "context required to understand"); subtitled; native-aspect for the destination platform (9:16 for Shorts/Reels/TikTok); hook ≤ 3 seconds |
| DoD-15 | **Newsletter** | Each edition has one core idea (not a link roundup); plain-text style preferred over heavily designed (B2B newsletter best practice); single CTA; open rate + reply rate tracked weekly |
| DoD-16 | **Webinar** | Topic chosen against client's documented sales objections; landing page + email sequence + LinkedIn promotion all shipped ≥ 14 days ahead; recording repurposed into ≥ 2 derivative assets (clip + blog post) after the event |
| DoD-17 | **Lead magnet** | Solves a real ICP problem (validated against either ICP interview notes or competitor gap analysis — never "we made a checklist"); gated behind email opt-in with explicit consent; tied to a follow-up nurture sequence (not a one-time download) |
| DoD-18 | **CRM / marketing automation** | Lead scoring documented and matches client's actual sales process (interview-validated), not a generic template; workflows have monitored exit conditions (not infinite loops); MQL→SQL handoff has a written SLA |
| DoD-19 | **Attribution / pipeline analytics** | Multi-touch model documented (which touches count, in what weights, with what window); marketing → revenue trace runs at least monthly and lands in the client's monthly report; methodology is reviewed quarterly |
| DoD-20 | **Review-site presence** | Profile is complete and up to date (logo, description, integrations, pricing block if public); monthly outreach to happy customers requesting reviews uses templated-but-personalized copy (not a one-click button); negative reviews replied to within 5 business days |

## 6. Add-ons menu — sold separately, not bundled

These are **NOT** in any core tier. Quote per-client when relevant. Pricing is CBO's; this is scope only.

| Add-on | What it includes | Notes |
|--------|------------------|-------|
| **Google Business Profile + Local SEO** | GBP optimization, NAP citations, local schema, geo-grid rank tracking | For B2B clients with physical office presence that matters (agencies serving local, B2B with show-room/HQ events). Pure local B2C: not Beeond's ICP. |
| **Review & reputation management (Google reviews)** | Google review outreach + response management | Distinct from G2/Capterra in Scale tier. For B2B with consumer-style reviews (legal/professional services). |
| **Speed-to-lead automation (B2B reframed)** | Demo-request → SDR-style instant response, calendaring, qualifying form | More likely to be a Wave-2 productized offering when Adam's swarm matures. |
| **SMS lifecycle** | Transactional + lifecycle SMS, opt-in compliance | Rare in B2B; pitch only when client has an existing SMS list. |
| **ABM program** | Target account list, 1:few content, multi-touch outreach | For high-ACV (>$50K) clients only. Substantial scope; quote per-engagement. |
| **Podcast / audio production** | Show production, guest booking, repurpose pipeline | One per quarter feasible at Scale; standalone otherwise. |
| **Community building** | Slack/Circle/Discord setup + facilitation | High-touch; only when client's strategy genuinely needs it. |
| **HE↔EN bilingual content parity** | First-language quality in both languages, in parallel | Built into core for Israeli clients. Add-on for clients who want a third language or who add the second language mid-engagement. |
| **Extra paid-ad creative pack** | +4 creative variants/mo beyond tier default | For clients running >$10K/mo in ad spend who burn creatives quickly. |
| **Custom dashboarding (Looker / GA4 / HubSpot)** | Bespoke reporting beyond template | When client has unusual reporting needs or board-level monthly readouts. |

## 7. Dependencies & open questions

### Founder must answer before this spec is communicated externally

1. **Two warm prospects — exact scope of need.** Without their actual marketing maturity, current channels, and pain points, we can't confirm which tier they'd pitch into. The Starter/Growth split in particular depends on whether either prospect currently runs paid ads or has an email list. **Action: founders capture this in the next discovery call and update `USER-INSIGHTS.md`.**

2. **Service catalog sign-off.** This spec accepts the recommended cuts/adds from `FOUNDING_BRIEF.md` §6. Specifically: GBP/citations/NAP/SMS moved to add-ons; LinkedIn organic + digital PR + G2-style review presence + founder-led content + newsletter + CRM/automation + attribution added to core (Growth/Scale). Founders confirm or revise.

3. **Personal-brand content split between founders.** Spec assumes one founder is the primary personal-brand voice through Growth (likely Yarden), with Adam's voice activating at Scale. Confirm — or split differently.

4. **Founder dedicated to weekly call at Scale.** Spec names Yarden as account lead at Scale (`BUSINESS_MODEL.md`). Confirm — this is a load-bearing commitment of her hours.

5. **YouTube production model.** Spec assumes Beeond does strategy + scripting + production coordination, not in-house shooting/editing. If client wants Beeond to own shoot+edit, that's a major scope addition or an explicit add-on (likely add-on).

6. **CRM/marketing-automation tool default.** Spec assumes HubSpot (per `BUSINESS_MODEL.md`). If clients arrive on Salesforce, Pipedrive, or ActiveCampaign, scope holds but implementation hours may differ. Confirm we'll support {HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, Pipedrive} — anything else is a custom quote.

7. **Stretch/contraction policy.** When a Growth client's needs swell (e.g., extra webinar), do we (a) bill an overrun, (b) absorb into the next month, or (c) hard-cap and defer? Spec assumes (c) hard-cap with deferral, but founders own this call.

8. **Pause / cancellation policy.** Spec is silent. CBO + founders must define before the FAQ section of the landing page is written (`POSITIONING.md` §5 Section 9 has a copy placeholder waiting on this).

### Cross-team dependencies

- **CBO:** This spec assumes the Starter/Growth/Scale price brackets are roughly correct against the tier's hours-load. If pricing shifts substantially (e.g., Growth halves), scope must come out — see §8 capacity flags.
- **CMO:** `POSITIONING.md` Section 5 (landing-page tier copy) needs this scope as input. CMO can now write tier-summary copy.
- **Design-Lead:** Section 5's "Services (Full Footprint)" cluster diagram should reflect the §3 service groupings.
- **CTO (Adam):** The capacity ceiling math (§8) depends on automation timeline. Adam's first automation targets should be the highest-hour services: SEO content drafting (~7.5–10 hrs/mo at Growth+), LinkedIn drafting (3–4 hrs/mo), monthly reporting (1.5–2 hrs/mo). A 30% hours-reduction on those three lines re-opens ~3–4 hrs/mo of buffer per client.

## 8. Capacity flags & honest constraints

| Flag | Detail | Impact |
|------|--------|--------|
| **F1 — Growth buffer is 0.5 hr** | Spec lands at 27.5 / 28 hrs at Growth. Any reactive month tips over. | **Action:** track per-client actuals from Day 1; renegotiate scope if 2 consecutive months >30 hrs. |
| **F2 — Scale at the ceiling, no buffer** | 50.0 / 50 hrs is precisely the ceiling. Zero margin for surprise. | **Action:** sell no more than 2 Scale clients concurrently until automation reduces hrs/line by ≥10% on top-3 services. |
| **F3 — Scale's third concurrent client is gated by automation** | Per `BUSINESS_MODEL.md` capacity math, 4 Scale clients = 200 hrs and exceeds the 220-hr deliverable budget when combined with other tiers. | **Action:** automation gates the move from "2 Scale concurrent" to "3+ Scale concurrent". Track delivery-hours per client weekly. |
| **F4 — Bilingual delivery doubles content hours** | Spec hours assume monolingual delivery. HE+EN parity content adds ~40–50% to content lines (SEO + LinkedIn + email + founder posts). | **Action:** HE+EN clients should be Growth+ tier with a documented bilingual scope addendum. Starter is monolingual only at full capacity. |
| **F5 — Phase 1 onboarding is heavy** | Scale Phase 1 = 36 one-time hrs. Two Scale clients onboarding in the same month = 72 hrs of one-time work, in addition to ongoing delivery. | **Action:** stagger Scale onboardings ≥ 4 weeks apart. |
| **F6 — Email lifecycle assumes existing list** | Email at Growth/Scale assumes the client already has a list to email. Growing the list from zero (newsletter at Scale helps) is a separate motion, not in monthly cadence. | **Action:** if client has <500 contacts, set explicit "list-building first" expectation in Phase 1. |
| **F7 — Paid ads cap on spend management** | Spec hours assume <$10K/mo ad spend management. >$10K/mo justifies the "Extra paid-ad creative pack" add-on at minimum. | **Action:** ad-spend thresholds trigger scope re-quote at $10K and $25K/mo client spend. |

## 9. Tier fit summary

| Tier | Hours fit | Buffer | Bilingual-safe? | Sellable now? |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Starter | 10.0 / 10 | 0 hrs | Monolingual only | Yes |
| Growth | 27.5 / 28 | 0.5 hrs | Yes (with bilingual addendum) | Yes |

**Verdict: all three tiers fit their founder-hour ceilings, with the honest caveats above.** Growth is the primary sellable tier as `BUSINESS_MODEL.md` predicts. Scale is sellable but capacity-gated. Starter exists for early-stage clients who would otherwise churn out of Growth pricing.
