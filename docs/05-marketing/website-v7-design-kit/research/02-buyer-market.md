# Buyer Truth — Desk Research (Phase 1 Strategy)

**Question:** What is the buyer truth that must ground the site's message?
**Method:** Desk research only (WebSearch/WebFetch), no customer interviews — per the 2026-07-08 process-runbook decision. Treat all figures as directional; many "AI adoption" stats below come from AI-search/GEO vendors with a commercial interest in the narrative (noted per row).

## 1. Buyer pains & JTBD (B2B/SaaS/tech decision-makers)

| Claim | Source | Date | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 48% of B2B marketing leaders cite budget/headcount/resource cuts as their #1 challenge; 59% of CMOs lack budget to execute strategy | prospeo.io/s/b2b-pain-points | 2026 | LOW (aggregator, primary study uncited) |
| ~90% of B2B teams report attribution challenges from fragmented/siloed data — hard to connect marketing to closed revenue | unboundb2b.com/blog/sales-and-marketing-problems-in-b2b-saas | 2026 | LOW (aggregator) |
| Avg B2B buying cycle ~10.1 months; buyers define purchase requirements before talking to sales 83% of the time; winning vendor is already on the Day-1 shortlist 95% of the time | corporatevisions.com (57 B2B buying-behavior stats) | 2026 | MEDIUM (large stat compilation, primary sources not individually verified) |
| Foundation AI models "minimize risk... suggest ideas in the most widely accepted ways," and once accepted as best practice this compounds into sameness across competitors | roboticmarketer.com | 2026 | LOW (single vendor blog, directional only) |

**JTBD synthesis (inferred, not directly quoted from buyers — UNKNOWN pending real interviews):**
- *When* our small marketing team can't keep pace with the channels we're supposed to own (content, SEO, ads, social, now AI-search), *we want* one accountable partner to run the whole footprint end-to-end, *so we can* stop context-switching between freelancers/tools and see pipeline move.
- *When* we try AI tools ourselves and the output reads like everyone else's, *we want* a partner who pairs automation with real brand/creative judgment, *so we can* ship fast without sounding like a template.
- *When* budget is cut but growth targets aren't, *we want* agency-grade output at a fraction of agency cost/timeline, *so we can* prove the marketing motion works before hiring in-house.

Direct SMB-founder verbatims ("our AI marketing sounds generic") were not found in named, dated, quotable form — that specific phrasing remains UNKNOWN/anecdotal.

| Claim | Source | Date | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 73% of B2B buyers use AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.) in purchase research, per an analysis of 680M AI citations across 6 studies (Oct 2025–Mar 2026) | Loganix/Averi, via PRNewswire | 2026-04-02 | LOW (vendor-commissioned meta-analysis; GEO/SEO vendor has commercial interest in this narrative) |

| 94% of B2B buyers reportedly use LLMs before contacting sales | testimonialstar.com | 2026 | LOW (vendor blog selling social-proof/testimonial tooling; self-interested) |

**Read:** Individual numbers are LOW confidence — the "AI-search stats" ecosystem is dominated by GEO/SEO vendors publishing self-serving figures to rank for these queries (a bias worth naming explicitly). But the *directional* claim — that a meaningful and growing share of B2B buyers now touch an AI answer engine somewhere in vendor research — is corroborated across HubSpot (content-differentiation angle), McKinsey (adoption baseline), and multiple independent vendor studies, which raises overall directional confidence to MEDIUM even though no single number is verifiable as stated.
