## The clichés — what NOT to say

Recurring near-verbatim across 8+ of the 11 players:

1. **"AI-native" / "AI-powered" / "AI agents"** as the headline noun — used by 7/11 players almost interchangeably with no differentiation left in the phrase itself.
2. **"Be the answer" / "get cited by AI" / "AI search visibility"** — the entire GEO bucket (5/5 players) converges on this exact framing. *(Flag: Beeond's own draft VP1 — "Be the answer when your buyer asks an AI" — is not differentiated; it's the category's stock phrase, verbatim on Surfer's hero.)*
3. **"One platform, not X point solutions"** — Copy.ai's core pitch; structurally identical to Beeond's "not three vendors and a Slack thread." Same claim, different noun (software vs. agency) — still a cliché shape.
4. **Big-number stats as the only trust currency**: "10,000+ teams", "$1B+ influenced", "30,000+ users", visibility-lift %. Every player leads with scale it doesn't have pre-revenue — a trap for Beeond.
5. **Enterprise logo walls** (Amazon, Boeing, Coinbase, Nike) — universal proof pattern, categorically unavailable to Beeond at launch.
6. **"Scale without adding headcount"** — Single Grain, Copy.ai, Jasper all use a version of this.
7. **Squad/pod language** ("growth squad", "Growth Lead", "agentic-marketer pod") — increasingly generic dressing on "you get a team."
8. **G2/Trustpilot badge rows** — near-universal, unusable pre-revenue.

## White space Beeond can credibly own

- **Whole footprint as ONE coordinated system, not a channel tool.** Every bucket-2/3 player is a point solution (content, or GEO-tracking) bolted onto a stack the buyer still assembles themselves. Bucket-1 agencies are broad but position as scaled "squads" serving enterprise logos — not a tight two-founder system. Nobody credibly claims "your entire footprint, one swarm, two people who built the machine."
- **Named, personified mechanism (the swarm/hive) vs. generic "agents."** "100+ AI agents" (Jasper) and "marketing agents" (Profound) are functional nouns; nobody has a brand-native metaphor with an identity system behind it. This is genuinely uncontested territory.
- **Founder-is-the-proof, not logo-wall-is-the-proof.** Every player's trust model requires scale Beeond doesn't have yet. A stated, deliberate "we're two people, ask us anything, we're selective" posture is unclaimed and turns the absence of logos into a feature.
- **Bilingual HE+EN, built native (not translated).** Zero players scanned address this; it's a clean, structural differentiator with no competitive noise to cut through.
- **GEO as one instrument in the orchestra, not the whole pitch.** The entire bucket-3 category (5 of 11 players) *is* GEO — meaning "AI search visibility" alone is now a commoditized, crowded claim (confirms 2026-06-29 DECISIONS.md finding). Beeond should mention GEO as included, never as the headline hook.

## Proof patterns — usable vs. not, pre-revenue

- **Not usable now:** customer counts, $ revenue-influenced, logo walls, G2/Trustpilot ratings, case-study %-lifts — every one requires data Beeond doesn't have.
- **Usable now:** founder credentials stated with specificity (per POSITIONING.md §6); a real, inspectable audit artifact (the free footprint audit itself as a proof object); explicit "we're selective, talk to us directly" framing in place of scale.
