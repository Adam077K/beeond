## The four founder picks

### 1 · Speakeasy — `speakeasy/` ⭐ FOUNDER PICK — the reference to orbit
- **Two versions in this folder.** `speakeasy-FOUNDER-PICK-fullpage.png` is the **previous "Craft exceptional API experiences" era** that the founder chose — **this is canonical.** The auto-captured `speakeasy-desktop-full.png` is the *current* "enterprise control plane" site — **ignore it.** Full teardown in `speakeasy/NOTES.md`.
- **Why it's the primary reference:** its structure already IS the Beeond structure — warm **cream ground with dark full-bleed showpiece sections**, huge **split editorial display headline**, a subtle **particle/swirl signature graphic** (almost literally a swarm) at hero + final CTA, and **product/artifact mocks as the imagery**. Restraint over loud color.
- **The Beeond move:** take Speakeasy's cream/dark rhythm + editorial restraint, then add the one thing it lacks — **honey-yellow warmth** — plus Hebrew-first display type and the swarm *meaning*. Premium bones, warm skin. See `NOTES.md` for the two decisions it forces (Hebrew display typeface; warmth-vs-gravitas balance).

### 2 · RZLT — `rzlt/`
- **Positioning:** *"GROWING THE BRANDS OF THE FUTURE."* — AI-native growth agency, proof-loaded.
- **Steal:** **stat-as-hero** — hard numbers (200M+ impressions, $100M+ funding) sit directly under the shouted headline. The metrics *are* the visual.
- **For Beeond:** our closest analog (an AI-native agency). When we have client numbers, this is the proof-strip model. Until then, our proof is founders + system scope, stated honestly.

### 3 · Jasper — `jasper/`
- **Positioning:** *"Put AI agents to work for marketing."* — near-identical category language to Beeond.
- **Steal:** **restrained palette, one accent** (navy/charcoal + Jasper-red confined to CTAs and key terms), content capped ~1200px, generous 60–100px gaps, sticky always-on CTA, role-based use-case carousel, customer stories with hard numbers. *"Get cited by AI, the new front door of search."*
- **For Beeond:** the direct competitor to out-craft. Read their copy to find what everyone says — then don't say it. Their red = our yellow discipline.

### 4 · Oriol — `oriol/`
- **Positioning:** *"Crafting lovable products for 15+ years."* — kind, human design leadership.
- **Steal:** **restraint as statement** — a tight red/green/yellow triad on huge whitespace; typography + color blocking *are* the imagery; zero illustration/3D. Mabry Pro × Aeonik Heavy.
- **For Beeond:** the blueprint for our **Editorial-restraint (C)** direction — the "less is more" play. Cheapest, best perf/a11y, and arguably the most confident.

## The pattern language (what all seven share)

1. **State a claim, not a tagline** — hero is a sentence with tension.
2. **Proof directly under the hero** — logos or hard numbers before features.
3. **One loud color** does the emotional work against a calm ground → for us, **`#FFDB5B` on cream**.
4. **One signature device** carried through the whole page.
5. **Alternate scannable capability cards with full-bleed proof** — density where it helps, air where it sells.
6. **Restraint everywhere the accent isn't.**

## Five transferable moves for cream + dark + yellow
1. Hero = a claim with tension (not a noun phrase). Hebrew display type at scale.
2. Proof strip immediately under it.
3. Yellow is the *only* loud color — scarce, intentional, a fill behind dark text.
4. Commit to one signature motion device; kill the rest.
5. Alternate capability tabs/cards with full-bleed proof sections.
