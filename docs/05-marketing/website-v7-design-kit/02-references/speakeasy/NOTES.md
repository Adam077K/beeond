## The five moves to steal (and how they become Beeond)

1. **Cream-primary with dark full-bleed showpiece sections.** This is the whole rhythm. Beeond has the exact tokens for it (`ground #FAF9F5` ↔ `deep #0A0A0A`). Our dark section = the **swarm showpiece** + the **social-proof/"who it's for"** moment.
2. **Split, oversized editorial display headline** as the hero — a claim broken across the canvas, not a centered tagline. **Beeond: in Hebrew display type, RTL.** (See the type note — this is the biggest open decision this reference forces.)
3. **One subtle particle/swirl signature graphic** at hero, resolving at the final CTA. → **This is almost literally a swarm.** For Beeond it's the honey/agent swarm as a restrained, abstract particle field — warm, alive, appearing once up top and resolving once at the close. Strong candidate to fuse "Editorial-restraint (C)" + "Swarm (A)" into the signature device.
4. **Product/UI mocks are the imagery** — no stock photos, no decorative illustration. Beeond isn't a dev tool, so our mocks are **artifacts**: the Footprint-Audit doc, a content calendar, a channel dashboard, an AI-answer citation. The artifacts double as the sales asset (as v6 already intuited).
5. **Restraint over loud color.** Speakeasy is nearly monochrome cream/ink. Its "wow" is *typography + the cream/dark rhythm + mock craft*, not color. → **Beeond's differentiator is exactly the color Speakeasy lacks: honey-yellow.** Layer `#FFDB5B` onto this restrained structure as the *one* warm accent (active card, CTA fill, one emphasis word, the particle glow). We get Speakeasy's elegance **plus** a warmth and energy it doesn't have. That gap is our brand.

## Two decisions this reference forces (for Session B)

- **Display typeface.** The premium feel here is largely the **light serif** display. Rubik (our current face) is a *friendly sans* with native Hebrew — elegant restraint is not its register. To land this reference's gravitas **Hebrew-first**, Session B must choose a **Hebrew display face** that carries editorial elegance (a refined Hebrew serif/contrast face for display, Rubik or similar for body), and prove it on the perf/a11y budget. This is now a top open type decision. Do not assume Rubik display.
- **Warmth vs. gravitas balance.** Speakeasy-liked reads *elegant/serious/premium*, less *warm/friendly/honey*. Beeond's brand brief is warm × futuristic × friendly. Recommended reading: **take Speakeasy's structure and restraint, keep Beeond's warmth via honey-yellow + rounder moments + human founder presence** — premium bones, warm skin. Founder should confirm this balance at gate B-a.
