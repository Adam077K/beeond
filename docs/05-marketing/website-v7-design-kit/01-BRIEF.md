# Beeond Website v7 — Design Brief

*The single source of truth for the reboot. Every session reads this first. Authored by CEO, 2026-07-07. Locked decisions from the founder are marked 🔒.*

---

## 0. The one-sentence mandate

> **Build the least generic, most crafted marketing site in the AI-agency category — because the site itself is the proof of the promise.**

Beeond's core promise answers the #1 fear SMBs have about AI marketing: *"it'll make us look generic."* The website is not a brochure for that promise — it **is** the promise, demonstrated. If the site looks like a template, we've lost before a word is read. Every craft decision is measured against one question: *would a template ever do this?*

## 1. What Beeond is (so the design tells the truth)

- **Beeond** = **Bee** + **(bey)ond**. A bilingual (Hebrew + English) **done-for-you marketing & growth agency** for B2B / SaaS / tech.
- It runs a client's **entire digital footprint** — every channel buyers use to find and evaluate vendors, *including the AIs they now ask* — as **one coordinated swarm of AI agents + two expert founders** (Yarden Morgan = marketing/growth; Adam = AI swarms/systems).
- The pitch is **agency-quality work at a speed and price no human agency can match**, because automation does the volume and humans do the taste.
- **Model:** tiered retainer + **phased onboarding** (~60–90 days: Foundation → Content engine → Amplification). Not a self-serve SaaS.
- **Stage:** pre-MVP, two warm unsigned customers (Israeli B2B likely). No signed revenue yet. The site's job is **lead generation**, not conversion-to-purchase.

**The metaphor, honestly used:** the hive/swarm moving as one toward a single goal — the client's footprint. Warm & natural (bee, hive, honey) × futuristic tech × friendly. *Not* cold sci-fi. *Not* a generic agency. "Future tech with people behind it."

## 3. Who we're talking to

Primary ICP: **B2B / SaaS / tech**, with initial pull toward **B2B service businesses & agencies** (a possible white-label/partner angle). Israeli-market-first (HE), global-capable (EN).

Three working personas (the design-planning session must pressure-test these with real customer names — the biggest open gap):
- **The B2B founder** — wears the marketing hat, no time, terrified of looking generic, wants a team without hiring one.
- **The solo/lean operator** — needs to "look bigger than headcount," ship consistently, and show up where buyers (and AIs) look.
- **The agency owner** — evaluating Beeond as a partner/white-label engine; judges craft ruthlessly.

What they must feel in 5 seconds: *"These people are clearly excellent, this is obviously not a template, and they can do for my brand what they've done for their own."*

## 4. The reference north star (see `02-references/`)

Four founder-chosen sites — Speakeasy, RZLT, Jasper, Oriol — plus curated cream/yellow/warm references (Mailchimp, Household, Basement). The **shared pattern language** the reboot adopts:

1. **State a claim, not a tagline.** The hero is a sentence with tension, not a noun phrase.
2. **Proof directly under the hero** — a row of concrete numbers/logos before any feature talk.
3. **One loud color** does all the emotional work — here, honey-yellow `#FFDB5B` — against a calm cream+dark ground (the Oriol/Jasper discipline).
4. **One signature motion device**, carried through the whole page — not scattered micro-animations (the Speakeasy discipline).
5. **Alternate scannable capability cards with full-bleed proof sections** (Speakeasy/Jasper rhythm).

We take the *pattern*, not the look. Speakeasy/Jasper are dark; **we are cream.** That contrast — a bold, high-craft, high-motion site that is *warm and light* — is itself a differentiator in a category drowning in dark-mode sameness.

## 5. The signature-move problem (the one big open creative decision)

Every reference has exactly **one** signature device. Beeond needs its own, and it must fuse: bee/swarm + AI agents + the "one goal / whole footprint" idea + warmth. The design-planning session should probe **3 candidate directions** (concept art via the OpenAI image pipeline, founder picks one — do **not** pre-lock this here):

- **A — "The Swarm" (living particles).** Hundreds of tiny yellow agents that coalesce on demand into a word, a hexagon, a chart, the client's mark — one motif everywhere. Warm + alive + literally "a swarm of agents." Risk: v6 already did "swarm→agents"; must feel abstract/new, not repeated.
- **B — "The Comb Grid" (structure, not decoration).** The honeycomb becomes the actual **layout grid** — content lives in hex cells; the yellow cell is the active/CTA one; the footprint "fills in" cell by cell. Structural, ownable, distinct from any ink illustration.
- **C — "Editorial restraint" (Oriol play).** Huge Hebrew display type, near-zero imagery, yellow as the single loud color, whitespace as luxury. Cheapest to build, best a11y/perf, strongest "less is more." The signature is *typographic confidence*, not an effect.

A hybrid is likely (e.g. C as the base with A or B as one hero showpiece). This is the highest-leverage creative call and belongs to the ideation session + founder.

**Strong signal (founder, 2026-07-07):** the founder supplied the *specific* Speakeasy version they like — the previous *"Craft exceptional API experiences"* era (`02-references/speakeasy/speakeasy-FOUNDER-PICK-fullpage.png` + `NOTES.md`). It points hard at a **hybrid of C + A**: cream-primary ground with **dark full-bleed showpiece sections**, an **oversized split editorial display headline**, a **subtle particle/swirl signature graphic** (a restrained, abstract swarm) that debuts at the hero and resolves at the final CTA, and **artifact/product mocks as the imagery** — all under Speakeasy's discipline of *restraint over loud color*. Beeond's differentiator is the one color Speakeasy omits: **honey-yellow `#FFDB5B`**, layered onto that restrained structure as the single warm accent. Two decisions this forces for Session B: (1) a **Hebrew display typeface** with editorial elegance (Speakeasy's gravitas comes from a light serif — Rubik is a friendly sans; do not assume Rubik for display), and (2) the **warmth-vs-gravitas balance** (recommendation: premium bones, warm skin — confirm at gate B-a).

## 7. What success looks like (the bar the build is measured against)

**Craft gates (inherited, non-negotiable):**
- Lighthouse mobile: **Perf ≥ 90 · A11y ≥ 95 · Best-practices 100 · SEO 100** (constitution invariant).
- Real-device **LCP < 900ms**, CLS < 0.01.
- **WCAG AA** — axe scan zero serious/critical. Yellow `#FFDB5B` is a **fill behind dark text only**, never text on cream (fails contrast).
- **Full RTL + LTR parity** — both SSR'd, CSS-toggled, zero layout breakage at 4 breakpoints.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` → rested end-state from first paint. JS-off → readable.
- e2e green; `brand-lint` clean (palette locked to the 7 tokens + any net-new token justified in `04-BRAND-CONSTRAINTS.md`).

**Outcome gates (the point of the site):**
- A visitor understands *what Beeond does* and *why it's not generic* within one screen.
- The **Footprint Audit CTA** is the obvious next step, present from the hero and repeated at the natural decision points.
- The onboarding wizard completes with a real, low-friction lead capture.
- The site is portfolio-grade: something the founders would show a prospective client as *"look what we build."*
