## R1 — independent verdicts (blind)

### Visionary — **KILL** (confidence: high)
> Building a marquee brochure before closing your first two customers is a 2018 agency move — in 2028's AI-discovery world, the category is won by whoever ships the most agent-citable proof-of-work, not the prettiest honeycomb site.

**Key findings:**
- **By 2028-30, B2B SaaS founders will not discover done-for-you agencies by browsing Hebrew marketing sites — they will ask an AI agent 'who runs whole-footprint for early-stage B2B SaaS' and the answer will be whoever has the highest volume of agent-legible, publicly-cited, receipt-bearing case artifacts. Beeond should be building the corpus that answer engines cite, not the brochure a human might visit.**
  - *Why it matters:* The proposed site is optimized for a discovery channel (direct human visit + share) that is contracting relative to the channel Beeond claims to specialize in (AI-answer surfaces). Building your own storefront while your ICP is migrating to concierge-by-LLM is orienting the storefront toward the shrinking half of the market.
- **The site is being asked to do a job that only delivered work can do — prove the argument 'we run whole-footprint as one system.' M5 is [FOUNDER INPUT NEEDED] because there IS no proof yet. A templated wizard reveal at the exact conversion moment is a tell: the site is a substitute for the product. No amount of Rubik Black + LCP <900ms fixes 'we have not yet done the thing we are selling.'**
  - *Why it matters:* You cannot brochure your way past pre-revenue. Every hour on the marquee is an hour not creating the artifact (a real delivered footprint) that would let the marquee actually land. This is the pre-revenue founder's most common self-deception — polishing the storefront because it is more comfortable than the sales call.
- **The M1-M6 hierarchy was locked without the warm-prospect interviews. This is not a soft spot — this is the whole game. Locking copy on assumed buyer language before you have heard your two warm prospects talk about their own pain in their own words means you are shipping a beautiful monument to your OWN theory of the buyer. If the theory is wrong, the site is not iterable-cheap — the visual system, the section arc, and the wizard's whole framing all rest on the same assumption.**
  - *Why it matters:* The 'locked' framing exists to protect founder momentum from feature-creep. It does not protect you from being wrong about the customer. Those are different failure modes and the second one kills companies.
- **Deliberately avoiding the 'AI-native / GEO / be-the-answer' category clichés is correct as differentiation copy but strategically inverted as positioning. The category clichés exist because that IS where the market attention is going in 2027-2029. 'Whole-footprint-as-one-system + founder-is-the-proof' is a lovely artisanal position — it is also the exact position that stays a two-person lifestyle shop and cannot scale past the founders' own personal bandwidth.**
  - *Why it matters:* Category-leadership positions require you to fight WHERE the category is forming, not adjacent to it. The safest positioning in a hot market — 'we are the calm grown-up alternative' — is historically the graveyard position: correct on taste, wrong on gravity.
- **The reversibility rating 'medium' understates it. The site is soft-iterable but the SEQUENCE choice — spend the Phase-3 window on marketing before revenue — is a founder-psychology commitment that is very hard to reverse. Once you have shipped a marquee site you will defend it, iterate it, and treat inbound-from-it as validation. The alternative path (ship the product, close the two warm prospects, let the receipts BE the marketing) becomes emotionally unavailable the day the honeycomb goes live.**
  - *Why it matters:* The biggest cost here is not the build hours — it is the identity lock. Two founders who define themselves as 'the ones who built the beautiful site' become worse at the harder unglamorous work of sitting on a call with an assumed-buyer and finding out the message is wrong.

**Outside-the-box alternative:** REVERSE THE SEQUENCE — ship a Public Audit Program instead of a marquee site. Concretely: (1) One-week build of ONE bare page + a REAL (not templated) footprint-audit tool that actually scans a domain across the AI-answer surfaces Beeond claims to run. (2) Publicly audit 10 named target-ICP B2B SaaS companies — with permission, or as unsolicited teardowns published in HE and EN. Each audit is a permalink artifact: methodology + findings + what a Beeond engagement would fix. (3) Send each audit directly to that company's founder as the cold-outbound message. This is your sales pipeline and your marketing corpus simultaneously. (4) The 'site' becomes a receipts-wall — 10, then 30, then 100 audits — the exact format AI-answer engines love to cite because it is dense, structured, named, and dated. (5) M5 credentials fill themselves: 'we have publicly audited 47 B2B SaaS footprints in 90 days' is a proof no honeycomb illustration can match. (6) The two warm prospects get closed on the phone in week one using an audit of THEIR company as the sales asset — if they do not close on that, the message is wrong and you find out for  worth of tool-build instead of  worth of marquee. This move is cheaper (one page, one tool), faster (revenue attempt in week one), bigger (each audit compounds SEO + AI-answer citation + credential), and structurally category-defining (nobody in the 11-competitor set is doing public receipts; they are all doing brochure sites). The founder-locked v7 site can still ship — in Phase 5, decorated with the receipts wall from Phase 3-4, and by then the M1-M6 hierarchy is written in customer language you actually heard.

**Kill condition:** If, within 60 days of a Public Audit Program launch, fewer than 3 of 10 audited companies engage in a discovery call AND the two existing warm prospects still do not close, then the message-market fit hypothesis is broken and the problem is not the storefront — it is the offer itself, and building any site (marquee or bare) would have been the wrong move regardless. Conversely: if the marquee site DOES ship and within 30 days generates >=5 qualified inbound leads in the target ICP without a single delivered audit or case study underneath it, the Visionary thesis is provably wrong — human-taste storefronts still convert cold in the AI-native SMB agency category and receipts-first is over-indexed on a 2028 world that has not arrived.

### Strategist — **PAUSE** (confidence: high)
> Wrong sequence: for a pre-revenue two-founder shop, the highest-leverage Phase-3 move is closing the two warm prospects and shipping the wizard as a standalone lead engine — not a full 8-section brochure built on unvalidated M1-M6 copy.

**Key findings:**
- **The build burns the scarcest resource (founder-weeks) on the lowest-EV asset. Two founders, zero revenue, two warm-but-uninterviewed prospects. A locked 8-section brochure + wizard + a11y/perf gates in one Fable-5 session is 1-2 founder-weeks of attention on a site that generates zero pipeline until traffic exists — while two hand-raisers sit un-contacted.**
  - *Why it matters:* In the 4-12 week Phase-3 window, the metric that matters is 'first paid engagement,' not 'Lighthouse 90/95/100/100.' Perfect brochure with 0 traffic beats 0 revenue by nothing.
- **M1-M6 is a bet-the-quarter copy lock on assumed buyer language. Seven of 11 competitors use near-identical AI-native/GEO clichés — Beeond's counter-position ('whole-footprint-as-one-system + founder-is-the-proof') is a sharp bet that ONLY works if the target buyer actually frames their pain that way. Zero interviews = zero evidence. If the wording is wrong you rebuild copy, sections, and the wizard reveal — the visual world is the only reusable asset.**
  - *Why it matters:* Reversibility is claimed 'medium — site is iterable,' but re-shooting copy after launch is the most expensive iteration mode (re-QA, re-a11y, re-RTL/LTR parity, re-JSON-LD). Better to spend 3 days validating before you spend 2 weeks building.
- **The wizard is doing the site's real job. Sections 1-8 are decoration around the ONE thing that captures a lead and demonstrates the argument: the Footprint-Audit. A standalone wizard at beeond.co/audit is 20% of the build, 80% of the conversion value, and 100% of the validation signal (buyers who complete Step 3 reveal their actual pain phrasing in Step 1 inputs).**
  - *Why it matters:* You get a shipped asset in ~1 week, real user language flowing back within days, and a de-risked spec for the full site if you still want it. The templated 'wow' problem gets tested for real instead of being an internal debate.
- **Founder credentials (M5) are still [FOUNDER INPUT NEEDED] — the ONLY real pre-revenue proof is empty. Shipping a marquee site whose central proof-panel is a placeholder is a category error. That paragraph is the single highest-leverage sentence on the entire site and it's not written.**
  - *Why it matters:* Launching without M5 filled means the site fails at its own thesis ('founder-is-the-proof') on day one. This is a hard block, not a soft spot.
- **Competitive consequence of delay is small; consequence of building-and-being-wrong is large. No competitor is winning Beeond's positioning THIS quarter — the 7-of-11 cliché cluster is a moat, not a threat. Losing 2 weeks to interview + wizard-only ship costs zero market position. Losing 2 weeks to build the wrong-copy full site burns the one shot at a category-defining first impression.**
  - *Why it matters:* Timing pressure is internal (founder locked the plan), not external (no competitor is about to take the position). Pausing to re-sequence has near-zero competitive downside.

**Conditions:**
- Week 1: Adam + Yarden run 30-min structured calls with BOTH warm prospects. Extract verbatim pain phrases. Compare to M1-M6 line by line. If ≥4 of 6 messages match their language → M1-M6 stands. If <4 → copy deck reopens BEFORE build.
- Week 1: Founder writes M5 credential paragraphs. No build starts while [FOUNDER INPUT NEEDED] persists.
- Weeks 2-3: Ship ONLY the Footprint-Audit wizard as a standalone route (beeond.co/audit) + minimal landing shell (hero + wizard CTA + M5 credentials + footer). Full a11y/perf/RTL parity gates apply. Full 8-section site DEFERRED to Phase 3.5.
- Weeks 3-4: Drive the two warm prospects + 20 cold-outbound targets through the wizard. Instrument Step 1 free-text inputs (pain phrasing), Step 3 reveal→email conversion, and post-audit call-book rate.
- Weeks 5-6 gate: Ship the full v7 site ONLY if wizard→booked-call rate ≥15% AND ≥1 paid engagement signed. Otherwise, the wizard IS the site and full-build stays deferred.
- One thing that makes it a category-defining asset (if built): the audit artifact must be publicly shareable by the recipient (unique URL, 'Audited by Beeond' badge, opt-in gallery of anonymized audits). The site sells because every audit becomes a backlink and a case study.

**Outside-the-box alternative:** Wizard-First, Brochure-Later — a 3-week play that reaches validated demand at ~20% of the build cost. WEEK 1: Two founder-led prospect interviews (verbatim capture) + M5 credentials written + copy-deck sanity check against real pain phrases. WEEK 2-3: Ship ONLY the Footprint-Audit wizard at beeond.co/audit, wrapped in a 3-block shell (hero one-liner, wizard, founder proof). Same visual world (Blueprint honeycomb), same Rubik, same HE/EN parity, same perf/a11y gates — 20% of the surface area, 100% of the conversion mechanic. The audit output becomes a shareable artifact ('Audited by Beeond' badge + unique URL) so every completed audit is a backlink, a referral trigger, and a live case study Beeond didn't have to fabricate. Success gate before building the full 8-section site: ≥15% wizard→call rate and ≥1 paid engagement. If it hits, Phase 3.5 builds the marquee site with VALIDATED copy and REAL customer quotes replacing the assumed M1-M6. If it misses, you learned in 3 weeks what a full-site launch would have taken 3 months to reveal. Net effect: the site the founder wants gets built — but on a foundation of paying customers and their actual words, which is the only version of that site worth the founder-week investment.

**Kill condition:** Within 30 days of any launch (wizard-only OR full site), zero booked discovery calls from wizard completions AND zero verbatim pain-phrase match between M1-M6 and the two warm-prospect interviews. That combination proves the copy lock was built on the wrong buyer model AND the wizard mechanic doesn't convert — meaning the entire Phase-3 sequence needed to be interviews-first, product-first, brochure-last.

### Architect — **PAUSE** (confidence: high)
> The site is trivially buildable on the locked stack — but a Fable-5 one-shot of an 8-section marquee is the wrong shape of build for a pre-revenue two-founder company; ship a 1-day proof-of-taste landing + a live-audit-thread instead.

**Key findings:**
- **Feasibility is not the constraint — sequencing is. Next.js 16 + Vercel + Supabase + Resend handles this spec in a normal week. The Lighthouse 90/95/100/100 + LCP <900ms + CLS <0.01 targets are achievable but tight given the drawn->built honeycomb signature device; expect 1-2 QA re-runs on mid-tier mobile LCP alone. This is a marketing-site build, not an architecture decision.**
  - *Why it matters:* The board is being asked to approve a build that doesn't actually stress the stack — which means the real question isn't 'can we?' but 'should we spend the Phase-3 window on it?'
- **Option A (full v7 as spec'd): ~$0-15/mo BOM delta (Vercel/Supabase/Resend already provisioned), 1 Fable-5 session + 2-3 QA cycles = ~1-2 weeks calendar, Risk MEDIUM (compressed one-shot amplifies re-run cost), Reversibility HARD on messaging/sequence (copy-locked in code, not CMS). Option B (thin proof-of-taste landing: hero + founder story + wizard-as-Typeform, HE only): ~$0/mo, 1-2 days, Risk LOW, Reversibility EASY. Option C (skip site entirely, ship 'public audit thread' — do the audit LIVE on 5 real sites, publish artifact + methodology as the marketing surface): ~$0/mo, 3-5 days, Risk LOW, Reversibility EASY, and it VALIDATES buyer language in the same motion.**
  - *Why it matters:* Option A's real cost is not tokens — it's the opportunity cost of the founders' Phase-3 window against two unvalidated warm prospects. Options B/C reach the same 'ranks in AI answers + artifact-as-proof' outcome with 5-10x less founder attention.
- **The wizard's Step-3 templated reveal is the load-bearing technical claim of the whole spec, and it is the weakest link. A heuristic reveal at the exact conversion moment is architecturally honest but conversion-fragile. If the honeypot is 'the audit artifact = pre-revenue proof,' the artifact needs to be demonstrably non-generic — templated output undercuts the M1-M6 argument that 'automation does volume, human taste keeps you non-generic.'**
  - *Why it matters:* The site's own thesis contradicts its own wizard. That's not a build problem — no code change fixes it. It's a spec problem the architect must surface before the build is sunk.
- **Copy-locked-in-code with no CMS means every future messaging iteration is a PR + QA-Full cycle. Given the M1-M6 hierarchy was locked without warm-prospect interviews, the probability of a post-launch copy pivot is high — and the current architecture makes that pivot expensive. Trivial mitigation on the locked stack: back copy with a Supabase table + ISR, or MDX with a stable schema. Cost: ~4 hours of build time, converts an irreversible bet into a reversible one.**
  - *Why it matters:* If we DO build Option A, this single architectural change (CMS-back the copy) is what turns 'medium reversibility' into 'genuinely soft' and is the cheapest insurance against the biggest known soft spot.

**Conditions:**
- Warm-prospect interviews (both, min 45 min each) completed BEFORE any copy-locked code is written — M1-M6 must survive contact with real buyer language, or be revised.
- If Option A proceeds: copy is Supabase/MDX-backed with a schema, NOT hard-coded strings — reversibility must be architected in, not promised.
- If Option A proceeds: build is scoped over 2 sessions, not 1 — session 1 = 8 sections + a11y/perf gates; session 2 = wizard + Resend + Supabase lead capture. Compressing both into one Fable-5 run is a QA-failure amplifier.
- Wizard Step-3 reveal must include at least ONE non-templated element sourced live (e.g., real fetch of the prospect's homepage title + og:image + h1 via Cloudflare Worker fetch) — pure heuristic reveal fails the 'human taste, not generic' thesis.
- Founder credential paragraphs (M5) filled in BEFORE build starts — building around [FOUNDER INPUT NEEDED] placeholders is how sites ship with placeholders.

**Outside-the-box alternative:** Ship a 1-day proof-of-taste landing (hero + founder story + a single 'Book a live audit' CTA, HE only, no wizard, no 8-section arc) and spend the rest of the Phase-3 window running LIVE audits on 5-10 real B2B/SaaS sites — the 2 warm prospects plus 3-8 strangers we cold-outreach. Publish each audit as a public artifact (Notion or MDX page, JSON-LD structured, discoverable by AI answer engines). The AUDIT THREAD becomes the marketing surface. This is architecturally cheaper (no wizard, no locked copy deck, no honeycomb device), 10x faster to signal (real artifacts vs. templated wizard output), and it validates M1-M6 buyer language IN THE SAME MOTION as generating proof-of-taste. The 8-section marquee site becomes Phase 4 — built AFTER we know which of M1-M6 actually resonates, funded by the first audit-to-retainer conversion. Same locked stack (Next.js + Vercel + MDX + Cloudflare Worker fetch for the live audits), ~$0/mo BOM delta, 3-5 days of founder time vs. 2 weeks. The audit artifacts double as the 'site itself sells' asset the founder is trying to build — because they ARE the sale.

**Kill condition:** Within 30 days of launch: either (a) zero warm-prospect meetings booked despite the site ranking in AI answers, proving M1-M6 messaging is wrong at the top of funnel — the entire copy-locked build becomes retroactively wasted; OR (b) the wizard's templated Step-3 reveal produces a completion-to-lead conversion rate below 5%, proving the 'honesty-vs-underwhelm' tension resolved to underwhelm at the exact moment it had to dazzle. Either signal means the sequencing was wrong: we should have validated buyer language and audit-artifact quality on 5 real sites BEFORE sinking a Fable-5 session into the marquee.

### risk-modeler — **PROCEED_WITH_CONDITIONS** (confidence: high)

**Kill condition:** Within 30 days of launch: fewer than 3 unique domain submissions to the wizard from non-founder-network traffic AND neither warm prospect has advanced to a paid engagement AND the M1 hero line does not appear verbatim (or near-verbatim) in any prospect's own description of their pain when asked 'why did you book a call.'
