## 1. Category eyebrow (sits above the locked H1)

Constraint: ≤5 words, brand-voiced (direct/confident/warm/anti-generic — no banned words, no emoji), **names the category** so a stranger passes the 5-second occlusion test. Renders small, Rubik 500, above "The AI era moves fast. Your presence needs to match."

| # | Option | Read |
|---|--------|------|
| A ⭐ | **Full-footprint growth, AI-native** | Names the category (growth/marketing, done for you across the whole footprint) + the differentiator. Clearest occlusion pass; unmistakably Beeond. |
| B | **Your whole footprint. One swarm.** | Leads with the owned mechanism/motif — most brand-distinctive, but names *what it does* more than *what it is* (relies on the subhead to land "agency"). |
| C | **Done-for-you growth, AI-native** | Plainest "what it is" — a skeptic instantly knows it's a DFY growth partner. Slightly more conventional. |
| D | **AI-native. Full-footprint. Founder-led.** | Three-beat brand cadence; punchy and trust-forward (founder-led), but three fragments can read as a slogan more than a category line. |
| E | **The AI-native growth agency** | Most literal category label. Safest for comprehension; riskiest for "AngelList descriptor" flatness the board warned about. |

**CEO recommendation: A** (occlusion-clear + on-brand), with **B** as the bolder alternative if you want the swarm motif to lead. **Founder approves the exact string before the session.**

## 2. Founder microlines (credential-dense — fill with REAL facts)

The board is blunt: *"restraint has nowhere to hide weak copy."* Generic ("AI systems" / "growth") reads as filler. Each founder line must make a skeptic think *"okay, these people know what they're doing."* Fill the brackets with specific, true, verifiable facts.

**Structure (per founder):**
> **[Name]** — [specific craft in 2-4 words]. Before Beeond, [the single most credible fact: a company/system built + scale or outcome, e.g. "built the agent swarm that did X for Y," "grew Z from A to B," a named client, a shipped product, a number]. [Optional second fact if it adds a *different* kind of proof.]
> Pull-quote (first person, specific, no buzzwords): "[one sentence that proves competence through a concrete claim, not an adjective]."
