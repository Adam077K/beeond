## 1. Positioning statement

> For **B2B/SaaS founders and lean marketing teams** (HE+EN) who need to be found and chosen everywhere buyers now evaluate them — search, social, review sites, and AI answers — **Beeond runs your entire digital footprint as one coordinated swarm of AI agents and two expert founders**, unlike traditional agencies (slow, siloed, five figures a month) or DIY AI tools (fast but generic), **because the founders who built the machine also run your account — automation does the volume, human taste keeps you from sounding like everyone else.**

**Check vs POSITIONING.md — AMENDMENT, not confirmation.** Two changes:
1. **Demote "AI-native" from the lead.** POSITIONING.md's statement leads with "AI-native done-for-you agency." Research 01 shows "AI-native" is used near-verbatim by 7/11 competitors — it is now category wallpaper, not a differentiator. Beeond *is* AI-native; it must not *say it first*. The lead is **whole-footprint-as-one-system + founder-is-the-machine**; AI-native is the how, stated later.
2. **Kill the inherited hero and VP1 line.** The "AI era moves fast / be the answer when your buyer asks an AI" framing (POSITIONING §5 hero + VP1) is the GEO category's stock phrase — "Be the answer" is verbatim on Surfer SEO's hero (research 01, row Surfer + cliché #2). Retire it. The pillars, voice canon, and anti-generic promise in POSITIONING.md are confirmed and carried forward.

## 2. Message hierarchy (the 6 messages the site argues, in order)

Each = a MESSAGE (not final copy). Proof is pre-revenue-honest. Cliché-to-avoid cites research 01.

**M1 — The evaluation moved, and it's fragmented.**
Buyers now judge you across many surfaces at once — search, LinkedIn, review sites, and increasingly AI answers — and most companies are present on almost none of them coherently.
- *Proof available:* the trend, stated qualitatively ("buyers increasingly research vendors with AI") — never a borrowed percentage. Directional confidence MEDIUM (research 02 §4).
- *Objection answered:* "Is this a real shift or vendor hype?"
- *Cliché to avoid:* leading with "AI search visibility" / big adoption stats (research 01 clichés #2, #4). Claim the trend, never the vendor number.

**M2 — Doing a fraction of it, and losing to those who aren't.**
The pain, sharp: scattered channels, no one accountable, pipeline left on the table.
- *Proof:* framed as the reader's situation, not a stat wall. Loss-aversion framing (marketing-psychology: Loss Aversion, decision stage).
- *Objection:* "Does this describe me?"
- *Cliché to avoid:* "one platform, not X point solutions" software-pitch shape (cliché #3).

**M3 — Beeond runs your WHOLE footprint as ONE system. (primary differentiator)**
Not a channel tool, not three vendors and a Slack thread — every channel coordinated toward one goal by one accountable team.
- *Proof:* the swarm/hive mechanism as a named, owned metaphor; the footprint map itself.
- *Objection:* "How is this different from a point tool or a scaled agency squad?"
- *Cliché to avoid:* generic "AI agents" / "growth squad / pod" language (clichés #1, #7). Use the branded swarm, not the functional noun.

**M4 — It won't make you generic — because humans own the taste. (the HIGH-confidence fear)**
Automation does the volume; the founders own brand voice and creative judgment; every flow is calibrated to your brand before it ships.
- *Proof:* the anti-generic mechanism, stated concretely; this is the only message backed by HIGH-confidence data (HubSpot 53%/52%, research 02 §3).
- *Objection:* "Isn't this just AI slop with a logo?" — the #1 market fear.
- *Cliché to avoid:* "no slop" as a bare claim (NoGood uses it) — Beeond must *show the split*, not assert the adjective.

**M5 — Founders are the proof — and we're bilingual-native.**
Two named people who built the machine deliver the work; HE and EN authored in parallel, neither a translation.
- *Proof:* founder credibility stated with specificity (POSITIONING §6); "we're two people, we're selective, ask us anything"; the site itself, in flawless HE+EN, as living proof.
- *Objection:* "No logos, no case studies — why trust you?" and "Will my Hebrew content be an afterthought?"
- *Cliché to avoid:* logo walls, G2 badge rows, "$1B+ influenced" (clichés #4, #5, #8) — all unavailable and all category-generic. Turn the absence of scale into a deliberate posture.

**M6 — The obvious, low-risk next step: your free Footprint Audit.**
See exactly where your footprint has gaps — value before any pitch.
- *Proof:* the audit is a real, inspectable artifact (risk-reversal, marketing-psychology decision stage).
- *Objection:* "What do I have to commit to?" — none; it's value-first.
- *Cliché to avoid:* "Get Started" / "Book a demo" generic CTA. The CTA is always *the Footprint Audit*.

*GEO note: it appears inside M1 and M3 as one instrument in the orchestra — never the headline hook (research 01 white-space: GEO is commoditized; 5/11 players ARE GEO).*

- ❌ "Be the answer when your buyer asks an AI" and near-variants (Surfer verbatim).
- ❌ "AI-native / AI-powered / AI agents" as the *headline noun* (7/11 players).

- ❌ Enterprise logo walls · G2/Trustpilot badge rows (pre-revenue, unavailable, category-universal).
- ❌ "Scale without adding headcount" · "growth squad / pod" language.
- ❌ POSITIONING.md's banned-word canon still applies: leverage, seamless, robust, best-in-class, cutting-edge, transformative, comprehensive.

**Angles ONLY Beeond can credibly claim:**

- A named, personified mechanism (the swarm/hive) with a real identity system — uncontested vs. generic "agents."

- Bilingual HE+EN built native — zero competitors scanned address this.
- The site itself as proof: it must *rank in AI answers and read flawlessly in both languages* — practicing what it sells.

## 5. Footprint-Audit wizard flow (structure + intent only)

The locked CTA destination. Feels like the *first taste of the product*, not a contact form. RTL-native; progresses right-to-left in HE. Structure only — microcopy is Phase 2.

- **Step 1 — The ask (1 field):** brand URL (or name). Intent: lowest-friction entry; single field. Reveals nothing yet; earns the next step.
- **Step 2 — Quick context (2–3 optional-feeling fields):** industry · rough size · primary goal (more leads / more visibility / ship faster). Intent: qualify + personalize the reveal. Skippable where honest; progress indicator.
- **Step 3 — The reveal (the wow, honest lean-real):** a **templated/heuristic** footprint snapshot for *their* brand — channels scored, gaps highlighted, "here's what it'd take to close them." **The honesty rule:** it is real and specific (computed from their inputs + observable signals), never a faked live-agent dashboard. Specificity + honesty beats fake precision — this *is* the anti-generic promise enacted. Live agent-scan is deferred (runbook v1 scope).
- **Step 4 — Capture (the exchange):** "Want the full audit + a 30-min plan with Yarden & Adam?" → name · work email → real lead capture (writes to a live destination; no dead mailto). Confirmation state sets expectations of what happens next.
- **Escape hatch:** a "just talk to the founders / skip to booking" path at any step, for people who buy people not process.
- **Lead honesty:** if they bounce at Step 4, URL+context already = a warm lead. States to design (Phase 2/3): empty · in-progress · loading-reveal · success · error (bad URL/network) · reduced-scope booking path. Full-tier QA surface (form + data capture).

**Must NOT fake:**

- ❌ Logo walls, G2/Trustpilot badges, testimonials we don't have.
- ❌ A fake "live scan" in the wizard. Faking scale backfires (research 02 §2) and detonates the anti-generic promise on contact.
