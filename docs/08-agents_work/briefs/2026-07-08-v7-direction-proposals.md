# Beeond Website v7 — Direction Proposals (Gate 2)

## A. The design problem

Build a site with Speakeasy-caliber restraint and its cream↔dark editorial rhythm, but Hebrew-first and honey-warm where Speakeasy is cool and Latin. It must argue the Gate-1 six-message case — hero states **M1 as a whole-picture claim** (founder ruling: no GEO/single-service focus), a dark **swarm showpiece** carries M3, a dark **final CTA** carries M6, across the 8-section arc — using image-gen-led art (generated composition + deterministic brand post-process) on exactly **7 locked tokens** (cream `#FAF9F5`, ink `#141413`, yellow `#FFDB5B` fill-only, panel, deep `#0A0A0A`, hairline, muted), Rubik body, one signature motion device. The live tension the three new RTL captures confirm: **premium Hebrew reads through weight and scale, not serif** — all three (alefalefalef, D+A, Bezalel) lean bold/confident sans, none a contrast serif. Each direction below resolves the gravitas↔warmth dial and the serif-vs-bold-sans question differently.

---

## B. Three directions

### A · "Quiet Authority" — the gravitas pole
**Soul:** the most composed room in the category — an intelligence so sure of itself it never raises its voice.
1. **Hebrew display:** **Frank Ruhl Libre**, Light–Regular (variable), at huge display sizes — the only credible Hebrew analogue to Speakeasy's light serif. Rubik stays body/UI. This is the one direction that *pursues* the serif ambition, against the research grain (see risks).
2. **Layout:** faithful Speakeasy — the hero claim **split across the canvas** (fragment top-right, fragment lower-left, RTL reading order), heavy macro-whitespace, cream primary with two dark full-bleed showpieces. Air is the luxury signal.
3. **Yellow:** the scarcest of the three (~5%) — a single emphasis word underlined, the CTA fill, one ember-glow in the dark swarm. Everything else is ink-on-cream.
4. **Signature device:** a **wispy monochrome particle vortex** (Speakeasy's swirl, reimagined as an agent swarm) — appears once upper-hero, resolves once at the dark final CTA. Near-monochrome; a faint honey warmth only as it resolves.
5. **Art program:** minimal-generated. One hero raster (the ink-particle swarm still, opaque, cream-ground), one dark-section texture; artifact mocks (Footprint-Audit doc, citation card) rendered as **crisp SVG/HTML**, not raster. Style keywords: *fine graphite particle field, editorial, matte paper, monochrome, restrained.* Lowest art budget, best perf.
6. **Hero states M1:** the fragmented-evaluation claim literally **broken across the viewport** — the split enacts the fragmentation; the swarm vortex sits between the fragments as the thing that reunifies them. Tension = a sentence you must scan across space to complete.
7. **Moodboard:** `speakeasy/speakeasy-FOUNDER-PICK-fullpage.png` (the whole rhythm + split serif hero); `oriol-desktop-hero.png` (restraint-as-statement, type is the art); `rtl/alefalefalef-desktop-full.png` — its **dark type-specimen panel** (proof a dark showpiece works in HE); `rtl/bezalel-desktop-hero.png` (restrained RTL sans hierarchy, single-accent discipline); Speakeasy dark MCP section (ornamental dark showpiece = our swarm moment).
8. **Risks:** highest. Frank Ruhl Libre at display fights the research finding that no premium HE site uses serif display — it can read *literary/old* rather than *futuristic*; mixed HE-serif + Latin-numerals in stat/URL strings clash (research §3). Least "warm × friendly" of the brief's three adjectives.

### B · "Warm Signal" — the balanced center *(recommended)*
**Soul:** Speakeasy's bones in a Hebrew-native bold voice — confident, warm, unmistakably built for Hebrew.
1. **Hebrew display:** **Rubik at its heaviest cut (Bold/Black)** driven to massive display scale — resolves the tension by siding with what all three RTL references actually do (weight+scale, not serif) and keeps everything on one already-locked family (zero second-face perf cost, no HE/Latin clash). Optional: Heebo Black as a display-only sibling if Rubik Black feels light at 120px+ — a net-new decision to prove on budget.
2. **Layout:** Speakeasy cream↔deep rhythm, but the hero headline is a **big confident bold block, RTL, with one asymmetric offset** (not a full four-corner split — bold sans wants mass, not scatter; the alefalefalef and D+A heroes prove this). Editorial mixed-scale grid (one large feature + smaller supports) for §5/§7, per D+A.
3. **Yellow:** the reference dose (~10%) used as **energy, not caution** (the Mailchimp lesson) — the active card, the CTA fill, one hero emphasis word on a honey underline/marker, the swarm's converging glow. Warm but disciplined.
4. **Signature device:** a **live SVG/canvas particle swarm** — scattered agent-dots in the hero that **converge into a single honeycomb/goal** at the M3 showpiece and resolve to one warm point at the dark final CTA. Code, not raster: sharper, lighter, animatable, reduced-motion rests as the converged end-state. This *is* the swarm meaning, not decoration.
5. **Art program:** hybrid, curated. Generated raster only for the **hero swarm-field texture** and the dark **swarm showpiece** backdrop; everything structural (comb map, artifact mocks, channel constellation) is SVG. Style keywords: *warm honey particle swarm, soft glow, matte cream paper, futuristic-but-organic, no lettering, opaque.* Post-process forces `#FAF9F5`/`#FFDB5B`. ~3–4 final rasters.
6. **Hero states M1:** a bold whole-picture claim held as one confident mass, with the swarm **scattered wide behind it** — the visual says "many surfaces, one system"; tension between the calm sentence and the not-yet-converged swarm behind it. Whole-picture, no single-service focus (founder ruling honored).
7. **Moodboard:** `speakeasy-FOUNDER-PICK-fullpage.png` (cream↔dark rhythm + dark showpiece); `rtl/alefalefalef-desktop-hero.png` (bold HE sans + cream + a black showpiece, *natively*); `rtl/da-magazine-desktop-hero.png` (large HE headline + mixed-scale editorial grid); `mailchimp-desktop-hero.png` (yellow as warm energy on cream, not caution); `rzlt-desktop-hero.png` (the swarm-of-nodes hero motif — take the *convergence idea*, drop the dark clutter); `rtl/bezalel-desktop-hero.png` (single-accent RTL discipline).
8. **Risks:** lowest-to-moderate. Bold Rubik at display risks reading *friendly-generic* rather than *premium* if the type scale and tracking aren't ruthlessly tuned (Hebrew reads heavier at equal px — research §3; tighten). Must earn gravitas through scale, spacing and craft, since it forgoes the serif's borrowed elegance.

### C · "The Living Hive" — the warmth pole
**Soul:** a warm organism you want to belong to — the friendliest, most alive version.
1. **Hebrew display:** **Rubik Black**, rounder and larger still, tracking loosened for a hand-warm feel; playful mixed-weight lines within one headline.
2. **Layout:** cream-dominant, softer — rounded panel insets, generous honey `panel` zoning, fewer hard dark sections (dark reserved only for the swarm showpiece + final CTA). Warmth via roundness and honey fields rather than air.
3. **Yellow:** the most present (~15%, still fill-only) — honey `panel` washes behind sections, yellow active states, honey glow throughout the swarm. The warmest, closest to a honeycomb feel; the direction that most tests the "keep it scarce" rule.
4. **Signature device:** a **honey/agent swarm that feels physical** — soft particle bees drifting in the hero, pooling into a glowing comb at M3, warming to a single honey-drop at the CTA. More generated-raster warmth than B.
5. **Art program:** the most generated art. Per-section honey-field textures + a hero swarm still + soft organic backdrops. Style keywords: *warm honey light, soft bokeh swarm, hand-crafted paper warmth, alive, organic, rounded, no lettering.* Highest art density → highest perf/curation cost. Artifact mocks still SVG.
6. **Hero states M1:** the whole footprint as a **warm living field** the reader is currently outside of — the swarm is alive and coherent, "everywhere your buyers look," and the tension is invitation (belong to this) rather than exposure.
7. **Moodboard:** `mailchimp-desktop-hero.png` (warmth without childishness — the ceiling for friendly yellow); `rtl/da-magazine-desktop-full.png` — its **sage/illustrated warm block** (soft accent panel in native HE); `rtl/alefalefalef-desktop-hero.png` (the peach/coral warm accent blocks on cream); `speakeasy-FOUNDER-PICK-fullpage.png` (keep the restraint anchor so warmth doesn't tip to toy); `rzlt-desktop-hero.png` (living swarm-of-nodes energy).
8. **Risks:** highest warmth-vs-premium risk — 15% yellow + heavy honey fields can tip from *warm-premium* to *consumer-cute*, undercutting the founder-led B2B seriousness (M5); highest art budget and the hardest perf/a11y gate (LCP <900ms with per-section raster).

## C. Comparison

| Axis | A · Quiet Authority | B · Warm Signal *(rec)* | C · Living Hive |
|---|---|---|---|
| gravitas↔warmth | gravitas pole | balanced center | warmth pole |

## D. Recommendation

**Build B · "Warm Signal."** It is the only direction whose type decision is *confirmed by evidence rather than fighting it*: the strategy brief demands Hebrew-first, and all three premium RTL references we captured (alefalefalef — a type foundry — plus D+A and Bezalel) carry their gravitas through bold sans at scale, none through serif (research 03 §1). B keeps everything on locked Rubik (no second-face perf tax, no HE/Latin serif clash), lands the brief's *warm × futuristic × friendly* mandate via honey-yellow energy and a live convergent swarm that literally enacts M3's whole-footprint-as-one-system claim, and preserves Speakeasy's cream↔dark editorial bones and the dark showpiece/final-CTA rhythm the strategy assigns to M3 and M6. It also carries the lowest build and perf risk against the non-negotiable gates. Direction A's serif elegance is the tempting one, but it is precisely the bet the RTL research warns against; C is worth keeping as the warmth reference B can borrow one degree from if the founder wants it friendlier.
