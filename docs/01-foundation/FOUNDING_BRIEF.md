---

## 1. Snapshot

**Beeond** is a **bilingual (Hebrew + English) done-for-you marketing & growth agency for B2B / SaaS / tech companies**, that runs a client's **entire digital footprint** as a single coordinated **swarm of AI agents + two expert founders**, delivering agency-quality work at a speed and price no traditional human agency can match.

- **Stage:** Idea + a couple of warm, unsigned customers. No brand, site, offer, pricing, or product yet.
- **Team:** Yarden Morgan (marketing & growth) + Adam (AI agents / swarms / systems). Two founders, no employees.
- **First milestone:** Foundation + docs in place (this week). First execution after: branding + landing page.
- **Budget posture:** Bootstrapped & lean — founders' time is the main resource; automate to save cost.

---

## 2. The name & the metaphor

**Beeond** = **Bee** + **(bey)ond**.
- **Bee / swarm:** Wild bees move as a coordinated swarm toward one goal in the hive. Beeond's agents, agent-swarms, and humans all work together toward **one goal: the client's digital brand / footprint.**
- **Beyond:** Signals the future of how marketing gets done — AI agents that lower price, ship faster, and adapt faster than any agency before.

## 4. Vision & positioning (one-liner candidates for CMO to refine)

- "Beeond runs your entire digital footprint with a swarm of AI agents — agency quality, at a speed and price agencies can't touch."
- "One swarm. One goal: your brand everywhere your customers — and the AIs they ask — are looking."

**Positioning pillars:**
1. **Whole footprint, one swarm** — not a point tool, not a siloed agency. Everything coordinated toward one goal.
2. **AI-native, founder-led** — built and run by people who do AI swarms (Adam) and marketing/growth (Yarden), not an agency bolting ChatGPT onto old workflows.
3. **Speed & price disruption** — automation collapses the cost and turnaround of agency-grade work.
4. **Not generic** — directly answers the #1 SMB fear of AI ("it'll look generic"): human creative judgment + brand-specific brainstorming sit on top of the automation.

## 5. ICP & customers

- **Primary ICP:** B2B / SaaS / tech companies. Initial pull is toward **B2B service businesses and agencies** (possible white-label / partner angle worth exploring).
- **Language:** Hebrew + English.
- **Current customers:** Two — **warm & likely, scope undefined.** ⚠️ **Names, industries, sizes, and needs are not yet captured — biggest open gap.** (See §11.)
- **The core customer insight to honor:** SMBs fear AI marketing because they think it'll look generic. Beeond's promise is the opposite — automation does the volume, humans do the taste and the brand-specific creative thinking.

## 6. The offer — "whole footprint," made deliverable

The founder rejected a single wedge. To sell and deliver the whole footprint without chaos, the model is **tiered packages + phased onboarding**:

- **Tiers** (names TBD, e.g. Starter / Growth / Scale): each covers the footprint at increasing depth.
- **Phased onboarding (~60–90 days):** Foundation → Content engine → Amplification. Do **not** light up 20 channels in week one.

### Service catalog — classified for the B2B/SaaS ICP

**CORE (the default footprint):**
GEO / AI-search visibility · SEO content engine · Schema automation · Rank tracking · Website build · Landing pages / CRO · Paid ads management · Social media (LinkedIn-led — see gaps) · Reddit & blogs · YouTube · Short-form video · Email lifecycle · Email deliverability · Reporting & dashboards · Session / funnel insight · Brand monitoring

**OPTIONAL ADD-ONS (offer only when relevant — mostly local-flavored):**
Google Business Profile · Reviews & reputation · Citations / NAP · Speed-to-lead & reminders (reframed for B2B inbound / demo requests) · SMS (B2B rarely needs it — email-first)

**RECOMMENDED CUTS / RE-FRAMES for B2B/SaaS:**
- GBP, Citations/NAP → pure local SEO. Keep as add-on only; not part of the B2B story.
- "Reviews & reputation" → for B2B this means **G2 / Capterra / Trustpilot + LinkedIn reputation**, not Google reviews. Re-scope it.
- "SMS" → trim from the email/SMS pairing; B2B lifecycle is email-led.

**MISSING — high-value for B2B/SaaS, not on the original list (recommend adding):**
- **LinkedIn organic + thought leadership** — the #1 B2B channel; "social media" undersells it.
- **Founder-led / personal branding** content (huge for B2B trust).
- **Digital PR + backlink / authority building** — directly powers both SEO rankings AND GEO citability.
- **Review-site presence (G2 / Capterra / Trustpilot)** — B2B's real "reviews."
- **Case studies & social-proof assets.**
- **Lead magnets / gated content / whitepapers.**
- **Webinars / virtual events.**
- **Marketing automation + CRM integration & lead nurture** (e.g. HubSpot) — turns traffic into pipeline.
- **Attribution / pipeline analytics** — tie marketing activity to revenue, not just "funnel insight."
- **Newsletter as an owned media asset.**
- *(Lower priority / optional: ABM for high-ACV targets, podcast/audio, community building.)*

## 7. Business model

- **Model:** Done-for-you agency → **recurring retainer** (recommended: tiered monthly retainer for predictable MRR + scope control). **Final pricing model and numbers are UNRESOLVED — owner: CBO.**
- **Cost structure:** Lean — founder time + low-cost tooling + automation. No payroll.
- **Capacity constraint:** Two founders manually delivering caps concurrent client count until automation lands. Pricing must reflect scarce founder time, not race-to-the-bottom.
- **CBO deliverables:** pricing model + tier definitions + price anchors (vs. cost of an in-house marketer / a traditional agency retainer) + a simple capacity/unit-economics model. Fill `BUSINESS_MODEL.md`.

## 8. Moat (honest assessment)

**Today, thin** — like any new agency. The plan to build it:
1. **Swarm tech (Adam):** proprietary agent-swarm orchestration that compounds — every codified flow lowers cost and raises speed vs. competitors.
2. **Founder pairing:** rare combination of real marketing/growth judgment (Yarden) + AI systems depth (Adam).
3. **Speed & price:** a wedge, not a moat alone (copyable) — defensible only when fused with the swarm tech and integrated full-footprint delivery.
4. **Integration / data:** running a client's *whole* footprint creates switching cost and a cross-channel data advantage over point-solution competitors.

*Detail to be expanded in `docs/02-competitive/MOAT.md` by Research-Lead / CBO.*

## 10. Delivery & ops model

- **Now:** mostly manual, AI-assisted, delivered by the two founders.
- **Path:** codify each delivery flow → progressively replace with agent swarms (Adam) → keep humans on strategy, creative judgment, brainstorming, and client comms (themselves automatable later).
- **Implication:** the first internal "build" is NOT a big platform. It's: the offer, the brand, a landing page, an onboarding flow, and the codified delivery playbooks Adam can later automate.
