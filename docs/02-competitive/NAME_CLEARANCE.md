# Name Clearance — Preliminary Screen: "Beeond" (marketing/advertising/growth agency)

> Research-Lead deliverable. Authored 2026-06-29. Markets screened: **Israel** and **US / English (international B2B)**.
>
> ⚠️ **THIS IS A PRELIMINARY COMMON-LAW + WEB SCREEN, NOT LEGAL CLEARANCE.** Official trademark registries (USPTO TESS/tmsearch, EUIPO/TMview, Israel ILPO, Brazil INPI/WIPO Global Brand DB) are JavaScript apps or bot-walled and could not be queried directly here. A registered-mark search and likelihood-of-confusion opinion require a **trademark attorney**. Do not treat "no exact match found" as "the name is clear."

---

## RISK VERDICT: **MEDIUM** collision risk

**Recommendation: PROCEED-WITH-CAUTION → get formal legal clearance before heavy brand investment.**

The name is *usable* and no exact-spelling, same-class, same-market blocker was found on this screen. But the namespace is **crowded and phonetically confusable in the exact services class (Nice Class 35 — advertising/marketing)**, which (a) raises likelihood-of-confusion hurdles to *registering and defending* "Beeond" as a trademark, and (b) creates real-world brand-confusion / SEO dilution risk. This is enough to warrant a trademark attorney's knockout search before Design-Lead invests in identity — but not enough to reconsider the name outright.

---

## Why MEDIUM (not LOW, not HIGH)

- **Not HIGH:** No exact-spelling "Beeond" mark operating *as a marketing agency in Israel or the US/EN B2B market* was found. The exact-spelling entities are in adjacent fields or non-target geographies.
- **Not LOW:** Trademark examiners (and courts) assess likelihood of confusion on **sight + sound + meaning + relatedness of services** — not exact spelling. "Beeond," "Beond," and "Beeyond" are **phonetically identical**, and several of these operate in **marketing/advertising (the same Class 35)**. An exact-spelling "Beeond" is *already used for advertising* (in Brazil). That combination makes the field genuinely contested.

## Per-market read

- **US / English (international B2B):** MEDIUM. No exact-spelling marketing-agency blocker found, but BeondX (worldwide) and Beeyond Media (NY/Miami) put a phonetically identical mark into Class 35 with US reach. A USPTO application for "Beeond" in Class 35 could face a likelihood-of-confusion refusal or opposition. **Attorney knockout search required.**
- **Israel (ILPO):** MEDIUM, but **largely a GAP** — the Israel registry could not be queried here. No Israeli "Beeond" marketing brand surfaced in web search, which is mildly reassuring, but absence of evidence ≠ clearance. **Must be checked by an Israeli TM attorney.**
- **Brazil/EU (context only, not target markets):** Beeond Publicidade (Brazil) is an exact-spelling in-advertising user; relevant only if Beeond ever expands there or they file via the Madrid Protocol. Flag, don't block.

## Recommendation (detail)

1. **PROCEED-WITH-CAUTION on the name** — it is not disqualified, and there is no exact-spelling, same-market, same-class registered blocker on the public web.
2. **Before any visual-identity investment, commission a trademark attorney** to run: (a) a USPTO knockout + full search in **Class 35 (advertising/marketing) and Class 42 (software)**; (b) an **Israel ILPO** search; (c) a likelihood-of-confusion read on **BeondX, Beeyond Media, and Beeond Publicidade**. Budget a few hundred to low-four-figures USD — cheap insurance vs. rebranding after launch.
3. **De-risk in parallel:** the brand will likely need a **distinctive house style + wordmark** (logo, color, the "bee/hive" device) to carve distinctiveness out of a crowded "Bee/Beyond/Beond" field — coordinate this with Design-Lead. A distinctive composite mark is more registrable than the bare word "Beeond."
4. **Defensive domain/handle posture** (see `DOMAIN_AVAILABILITY.md`) supports the name but does **not** equal trademark rights.
5. **Do NOT** rely on this document as clearance. Where the verdict turns on register status (gaps 1–6), **a trademark attorney is required.**
