## Personas

### 1. Maya — the B2B/SaaS founder-operator
- **Role:** Founder / CEO, often still owning marketing herself because there's no CMO yet.
- **Company size:** 5–30 people; seed to early-Series-A B2B SaaS; HE+EN market.
- **Primary pain:** Growth targets don't fall when budget does. She knows the company is under-present across the channels buyers now check (search, LinkedIn, G2, and increasingly AI answers) but has neither the time nor a trusted operator to run it. **[ASSUMPTION — desk research, no interviews, 2026-07-08]** — grounded in "48% of B2B leaders cite budget/headcount cuts as #1 challenge" (LOW-confidence aggregator) + the founder's warm-prospect read.
- **JTBD:** *When* my growth targets stay flat but I can't afford an in-house marketing team, *I want* one accountable partner to run my whole footprint end-to-end, *so I can* prove the motion works before I hire. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Budget posture:** Cost-conscious; balks at a five-figure/mo traditional retainer; open to paying for outcomes and founder access. Values "agency breadth without agency cost." **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Decision power:** Full — she is the buyer and the budget. Short path to yes, but high skepticism of vendor claims.
- **Churn trigger:** No visible pipeline movement inside the first 60–90 days; feeling like she's talking to a system, not the founders. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Expansion trigger:** First channel produces a real lead → she wants the next layer lit up (Foundation → Content → Amplification). **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**

### 2. Daniel — the lean-marketing operator
- **Role:** Head of Marketing / solo marketer / "marketing team of 1–3" inside a growing B2B/SaaS company.
- **Company size:** 20–80 people; Series A/B; a founder above him who wants results.
- **Primary pain:** He's accountable for more channels than one person can run, and every AI tool he tries produces output that "sounds like everyone else." He is drowning in coordination — freelancers, tools, a Slack thread nobody owns. **[ASSUMPTION — desk research, 2026-07-08]** — the generic-output half is grounded HIGH: HubSpot reports 53% of marketers struggle to make content stand out and 52% say AI has made content less effective overall.
- **JTBD:** *When* I can't keep pace with the channels I'm supposed to own and my AI experiments come out generic, *I want* a partner that pairs automation with real brand judgment, *so I can* ship fast without sounding like a template. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08; generic-fear half is HIGH-confidence]**
- **Budget posture:** Has a real but defended budget; must justify spend to the founder against "why not just hire a freelancer / buy a tool." Sensitive to being sold vaporware. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Decision power:** Recommender, not final signer — needs internal proof to sell it up. Champion-with-a-boss.
- **Churn trigger:** Output he can't confidently put his name on; work that reads AI-generic; slow turnaround that erases the speed promise. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Expansion trigger:** A piece of work his founder praises → he expands scope to defend his own headcount case. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**

### 3. Yossi — the agency owner (white-label angle)
- **Role:** Owner/principal of a small marketing or web agency serving B2B/local clients.
- **Company size:** 3–15 people; project- or retainer-based; HE-first client base.
- **Primary pain:** Client demand for AI-search, GEO, and always-on content outpaces his delivery capacity, and hiring specialists per channel destroys his margin. He needs a delivery engine he can put his own brand on. **[ASSUMPTION — desk research, no interviews, 2026-07-08]** — the founder brief flags "initial pull toward B2B service businesses & agencies" as the warmest segment; the white-label motive is inferred, not confirmed.
- **JTBD:** *When* clients ask for more channels than my team can profitably staff, *I want* a coordinated delivery swarm I can resell under my own brand, *so I can* widen my offer and margin without hiring. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Budget posture:** Thinks in margin and per-client unit economics, not retail price; wants wholesale/partner terms. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Decision power:** Full owner-buyer, but slower — he's underwriting his own clients' outcomes on Beeond's delivery, so trust bar is highest of the three. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Churn trigger:** Any delivery miss that reaches his client; loss of white-label control or brand exposure. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Expansion trigger:** One client win he can attribute to the swarm → rolls it across his book. **[ASSUMPTION — 2026-07-08]**
- **Note:** White-label is a *hypothesis worth a validation conversation*, not a confirmed offer. Flag for founder at Gate 1.
